Verb Conjugation in ENGLISH/LAKOTA Section:
1st cl. - wa/ya
action caused by subject
2nd cl. - bl/l
either/state of being
3rd cl. - ma/ni
action happened to subject
/ - indicates where the definite article is embedded in verb.
[y] - in the case of 2nd class verbs, this indicates that this “y” is the def. art. for “he/she/it” (and subsequently
transforms to “bl” for “I”, “l” for “you”, etc.).
For example:
The verb "to walk" is listed here in the following form:
"walk, to - v. má/ni (ma-nee) 1st cl."
This indicates that it is a 1st class verb, and should be conjugated like so (note that the "/" indicates where
the conjugated forms are inserted):
I walk:
we walk:
mawani
mauŋnipi
you walk:
you all walk:
mayani
mayanipi
he/she/it walks:
mani
they walk:
manipi
For 2nd class verbs (as well as a few 1st or 3rd class verbs) the "y" is replaced by the conjugated form.
Because of this, the dropped "y" is written in brackets:
"have, to - v. [y]uhá (yoo-ha) 2nd cl."
I have:
we have:
bluhá
uŋyuhápi
you have:
you all have:
luhá
luhápi
he/she/it has:
yuhá
they have:
yuhápi
