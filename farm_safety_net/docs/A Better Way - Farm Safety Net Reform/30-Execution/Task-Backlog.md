# Task Backlog

## Introduction

This task backlog provides a comprehensive inventory of work items required to successfully implement A Better Way: Farm Safety Net Reform Initiative over its four-year timeline. The backlog is organized around the two core initiatives—No Farm Lost and Rethinking the Safety Net—and aligned with the three-phase communications strategy. Tasks are prioritized to support immediate crisis response while building toward systemic policy reform.

## Phase 1 Tasks: Foundation and Crisis Response (Months 1-12)

### Project Infrastructure Setup

**Priority: Critical**
- Establish project management systems and communication protocols
- Finalize working group member commitments and participation agreements
- Create secure collaboration platforms for policy development activities
- Develop project branding and visual identity guidelines
- Set up legal and administrative framework for multi-organizational collaboration

### No Farm Lost Program Development

**Priority: Critical**
- Design and build resource hub website with crisis assistance information
- Develop farmer-to-farmer video content collection and production process
- Create standardized materials for organizations serving farmers in crisis
- Establish partnerships with farm credit counselors and financial advisors
- Design intake and referral system for farmers seeking assistance

### Initial Communications Infrastructure

**Priority: High**
- Develop messaging framework emphasizing farmer experience and financial crisis
- Create content calendar for farmer story collection and sharing
- Establish social media presence and engagement strategies
- Design "Join Us" campaign materials and commitment mechanisms
- Build email list and stakeholder database

### Working Group Formation

**Priority: High**
- Conduct first quarterly working group meeting and establish operating procedures
- Define policy development criteria and evaluation frameworks
- Assign research responsibilities among working group members
- Create knowledge sharing protocols and documentation standards
- Establish external expert consultation process

## Phase 2 Tasks: Analysis and Coalition Building (Months 13-24)

### Policy Research and Development

**Priority: Critical**
- Conduct comprehensive analysis of current safety net program impacts by farm scale
- Model alternative safety net frameworks based on six reform principles
- Develop case studies demonstrating policy impacts on different farm types
- Create policy briefs linking farmer stories to systemic reform needs
- Test policy models through research partnerships and pilot programs

### Strategic Partnership Development

**Priority: High**
- Build coalitions with family-scale commodity producer organizations
- Establish partnerships with sustainable and regenerative agriculture advocates
- Engage civil rights organizations focused on farm equity issues
- Connect with rural development and anti-poverty organizations
- Develop relationships with academic research institutions

### Enhanced Communications Activities

**Priority: High**
- Produce in-depth farmer story content with policy analysis backing
- Create educational materials explaining safety net program inequities
- Develop targeted messaging for different stakeholder audiences
- Launch speaker bureau for conferences and organizational presentations
- Expand media outreach to agricultural and policy publications

### Crisis Response Scaling

**Priority: Medium**
- Evaluate No Farm Lost program effectiveness and expand successful components
- Train additional organizations in crisis intervention protocols
- Develop specialized resources for farmers from underrepresented communities
- Create peer support networks for farmers in financial distress
- Establish emergency response protocols for acute crisis situations

## Phase 3 Tasks: Policy Release and Advocacy (Months 25-48)

### Policy Package Finalization

**Priority: Critical**
- Complete comprehensive safety net reform proposal with legislative language
- Develop implementation timeline and administrative guidance recommendations
- Create cost-benefit analysis and budget impact projections
- Prepare policy rollout materials for multiple stakeholder audiences
- Establish policy tracking and monitoring systems

### Legislative and Administrative Advocacy

**Priority: Critical**
- Coordinate policy introduction in Congress through allied representatives
- Engage USDA leadership on administrative reform opportunities
- Develop state-level implementation strategies and pilot programs
- Create advocacy toolkit for grassroots organizing efforts
- Establish coalition coordination for sustained advocacy campaign

### Public Education Campaign

**Priority: High**
- Launch comprehensive media campaign highlighting policy solutions
- Coordinate farmer testimony for congressional hearings and public forums
- Develop educational materials for agricultural extension and outreach programs
- Create digital campaign assets for social media and online organizing
- Establish ongoing policy analysis and commentary capacity

### Program Sustainability Planning

**Priority: Medium**
- Develop long-term funding strategy for continued crisis assistance
- Create organizational structure for ongoing policy advocacy
- Establish farmer leadership development and succession planning
- Design program evaluation and impact measurement systems
- Plan transition to permanent organizational structure

## Ongoing Tasks (All Phases)

### Quarterly Working Group Activities

**Priority: High**
- Conduct quarterly meetings with policy experts and advisors
- Maintain regular communication and collaboration between meetings
- Coordinate research activities and findings synthesis
- Review and refine policy development based on emerging evidence
- Adapt strategy based on changing political and economic conditions

### Crisis Response Operations

**Priority: Critical**
- Maintain 24/7 resource availability for farmers in financial crisis
- Update and expand crisis assistance materials based on user feedback
- Coordinate with farm credit and legal assistance providers
- Track and analyze crisis intervention outcomes and effectiveness
- Respond to emerging crisis situations and policy developments

### Communications and Engagement

**Priority: High**
- Maintain consistent content production and stakeholder engagement
- Monitor and respond to media coverage and public discourse
- Adapt messaging based on audience feedback and political developments
- Coordinate speaking engagements and public education opportunities
- Track and analyze engagement metrics and communication effectiveness

## Resource Requirements and Dependencies

### Critical Dependencies
- Working group member availability and sustained engagement
- Farmer willingness to share stories and participate in advocacy
- Access to policy research and analysis capabilities
- Coordination with existing farm assistance organizations
- Political window opportunities for policy introduction

### Success Metrics
- Number of farms assisted through crisis intervention
- Policy proposal completion and stakeholder endorsement
- Coalition size and diversity of supporting organizations
- Media coverage and public awareness of reform needs
- Legislative and administrative policy advancement

## Next Steps

The immediate priority is completing Phase 1 foundation tasks while maintaining crisis response capacity. Success in early phases builds credibility and coalition strength necessary for effective policy advocacy in Phase 3. Regular evaluation and adaptation of the task backlog ensures responsiveness to changing conditions while maintaining focus on the core mission of preventing farm loss and achieving systemic safety net reform.
