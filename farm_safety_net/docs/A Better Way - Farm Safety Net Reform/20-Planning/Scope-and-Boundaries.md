# Scope and Boundaries

## Introduction

This document defines the scope and boundaries for A Better Way: Farm Safety Net Reform Initiative, a four-year project addressing the most significant threat to farm viability since the 1980s farm crisis. Clear scope definition ensures focused execution of the initiative's dual approach while establishing explicit boundaries to prevent mission creep and maintain strategic focus. The scope encompasses both immediate crisis intervention through "No Farm Lost" and comprehensive policy reform through "Rethinking the Safety Net."

## Project Scope

### In-Scope Activities

#### No Farm Lost Initiative

**Crisis Response and Resource Development**
- Development and maintenance of comprehensive resource hub for farmers facing financial crisis
- Creation of farmer-to-farmer video content featuring survival stories and practical guidance
- Production of educational materials for organizations working directly with farmers in crisis
- Implementation of communications campaign emphasizing farmer ownership of land
- Execution of "Join Us" public commitment campaign supporting farmer ownership

**Communications and Outreach**
- Three-phase communications strategy spanning the four-year initiative timeline
- Development of messaging frameworks focusing on farmer experience and financial crisis
- Creation of engagement infrastructure for sustained stakeholder involvement
- Establishment of strategic partnerships across traditional political and agricultural divides

#### Rethinking the Safety Net Initiative

**Policy Development and Research**
- Quarterly working group meetings with invited policy experts
- Development of policy criteria based on six established principles for safety net reform
- Testing of policy models through research and practical casework
- Analysis of existing safety net programs including Market Facilitation Program (MFP) impacts

**Strategic Partnership Building**
- Cultivation of relationships with diverse agricultural, policy, and advocacy organizations
- Engagement with unlikely alliance partners across sustainable, regenerative, and commodity agriculture sectors
- Development of implementation pathways for policy recommendations

### Geographic Scope

**Primary Focus Areas**
- United States federal farm policy and safety net programs
- National-level policy analysis and reform recommendations
- Cross-regional farmer crisis intervention and resource provision

**Secondary Considerations**
- State-level policy implications and implementation pathways
- Regional variations in farm crisis impacts and safety net effectiveness
- Tribal agricultural operations and sovereignty considerations

### Temporal Scope

**Project Duration**
- Four-year initiative timeline from project inception
- Three distinct communications phases aligned with policy development milestones
- Quarterly working group meetings throughout project duration

**Crisis Response Timeline**
- Immediate resource deployment for farmers in financial danger
- Ongoing crisis intervention throughout project duration
- Long-term prevention strategies through policy reform

## Project Boundaries

### Explicit Exclusions

#### Direct Financial Assistance
- The project will not provide direct financial payments or loans to individual farmers
- No creation of new funding mechanisms or grant programs for farmer assistance
- No administration of existing federal or state financial assistance programs

#### Operational Farm Management
- No direct involvement in individual farm business planning or operational decisions
- No provision of technical assistance for specific agricultural practices
- No farm-level consulting services or business advisory functions

#### Regulatory Implementation
- No direct regulatory authority or enforcement responsibilities
- No implementation of federal or state agricultural regulations
- No oversight of existing safety net program administration

### Organizational Boundaries

#### Federal Agency Relationships
- Project will maintain independence from USDA and other federal agencies
- No formal partnership agreements with federal agencies during active policy development
- Engagement limited to information gathering and stakeholder consultation

#### Political Advocacy Limitations
- No direct lobbying activities requiring federal registration
- No partisan political endorsements or campaign activities
- No coordination with political campaigns or partisan organizations

#### Commercial Agricultural Interests
- No partnerships with agribusiness corporations or commodity trading entities
- No endorsement of specific agricultural products, services, or technologies
- No commercial consulting relationships with large-scale agricultural operations

### Resource Boundaries

#### Staffing Limitations
- Project Director and Senior Advisors as specified in project leadership structure
- Working group limited to invited policy experts as identified
- No expansion of core team beyond established organizational capacity

#### Financial Constraints
- Project activities limited to available funding for four-year initiative
- No creation of ongoing financial commitments beyond project timeline
- No assumption of liability for individual farmer financial outcomes

#### Technology and Platform Constraints
- Resource hub and communications limited to web-based platforms and traditional media
- No development of proprietary software or technology solutions
- No creation of complex database systems requiring ongoing technical maintenance

### Success Measurement Boundaries

#### Direct Attribution Limitations
- Project cannot claim direct attribution for individual farm survival outcomes
- Policy impact measurement limited to adoption and implementation tracking
- Communications effectiveness measured through engagement metrics rather than behavioral change attribution

#### Long-term Impact Assessment
- Four-year project timeline limits long-term impact evaluation
- Policy implementation success dependent on external political and economic factors
- Systemic change measurement extends beyond project completion timeline

## Interface Management

### Stakeholder Coordination
- Clear communication protocols with farmer organizations, policy groups, and advocacy partners
- Defined roles and responsibilities for working group members and senior advisors
- Established boundaries for media engagement and public representation

### Information Sharing
- Protocols for sharing research findings and policy recommendations
- Guidelines for attribution and credit in collaborative policy development
- Clear boundaries around proprietary analysis and strategic planning information

## Risk Mitigation Through Scope Management

### Mission Creep Prevention
- Regular scope review and boundary reinforcement throughout project duration
- Clear decision-making criteria for evaluating new opportunities or requests
- Documented process for scope change approval and stakeholder notification

### Resource Protection
- Explicit boundaries prevent overextension of project resources
- Clear priorities ensure focus on core deliverables and outcomes
- Defined limitations protect project sustainability and effectiveness

## Conclusion

These scope and boundaries provide essential guardrails for A Better Way: Farm Safety Net Reform Initiative, ensuring focused execution of crisis intervention and policy reform objectives while preventing mission expansion beyond organizational capacity. The defined scope enables meaningful impact on farm viability and safety net reform while maintaining strategic focus and resource discipline throughout the four-year initiative. Regular review and adherence to these boundaries will be critical for project success and sustainable impact on American agricultural policy.
